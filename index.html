About SparkBox

Learn about our mission to improve the department.

What is SparkBox?

SparkBox is the official suggestion and innovation platform for the Electronics & Computer Engineering Department at MIT College of Railway Engineering and Research, Barshi.

Why was it created?

To empower students to share their ideas openly, ensuring every voice is heard in our mission to build a better academic and physical environment.

How students can contribute?

Students can submit new ideas, vote on existing ones, and participate in monthly challenges. Contributions are recognized through XP and badges.

How the department reviews suggestions?

Faculty coordinators regularly review top-voted and highly relevant ideas, providing official feedback and updating statuses to keep students informed.

How implemented ideas improve the department?

Successful suggestions lead directly to upgraded labs, better events, refined curriculums, and a more engaging campus life.

Frequently Asked Questions

Got questions? We have answers.

Can I submit anonymously?

Yes! Just leave the "Student Name" field empty when submitting your idea.

How long does review take?

The faculty reviews top ideas bi-weekly. Highly voted ideas are prioritized.

Who reviews suggestions?

A dedicated panel of ECE faculty members and the Head of Department.

Can I edit my suggestion?

Currently, editing is not supported. Please review your idea carefully before submitting.

How are ideas selected?

Ideas are selected based on student votes, feasibility, and impact on the department's goals.
